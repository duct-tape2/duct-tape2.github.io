The Buyer-Ready Version

A storefront is ready when a stranger can understand the result, inspect a sample, pay safely, and know exactly how delivery or support will happen. Start with these pieces before adding more tools.

Step 1

Pick one paid outcome

Write the product name, price, file format, who it helps, and what the buyer gets within five minutes of opening the page.

Step 2

Show a real sample

Add a preview file, demo page, checklist, sample report, or small Anki deck. The sample should prove the paid item exists.

Step 3

Add payment and support

Use a checkout link, a support email, public-safe buyer help, and refund/support terms. Keep private payment details out of public issues.

Step 4

Check before sharing

Confirm HTTP 200, title, description, social image, payment CTA, support path, free sample, and delivery instructions.

Copy This Minimum Page Structure

  • Hero: one sentence promise, price, primary buy button, sample link.
  • What you get: exact files, page count, templates, decks, or service deliverables.
  • Free preview: one inspectable asset before payment.
  • After payment: delivery email, order note, and support boundary.
  • Refund/support terms: short, visible, and specific.
  • Public-safe issue form: for non-private buyer help only.
