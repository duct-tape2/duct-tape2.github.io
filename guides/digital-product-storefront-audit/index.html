Audit The Buyer Path

A small product page can lose sales even when the product is fine. The usual gaps are simple: unclear promise, no sample, payment amount ambiguity, no delivery email, no refund note, or no way to ask a public-safe question.

1. Offer

Name the exact result

Say what the buyer gets, who it is for, the file format, the price, and what is not promised. Avoid vague claims like passive income, guaranteed traffic, or instant ranking.

2. Evidence

Put a sample near the buy button

Link a preview ZIP, sample report, screenshot, APKG sample, or live example before the checkout button. Buyers should not have to trust a bare PayPal link.

3. Checkout

Make the amount and product unambiguous

PayPal.Me shows the amount, not always the product. Add an email note button asking for offer name, payer name or email prefix, approximate payment time, and delivery email.

4. Aftercare

Show support and refund terms

A short refund/support line reduces risk for the buyer and prevents avoidable disputes. Keep private payment details out of public GitHub issues.

Fast Fixes

  • If there is no clear product page, use the free static storefront builder first.
  • If the payment link is missing, build a PayPal checkout block and add it near the sample.
  • If the product is ready but the page feels risky, get a written storefront review.
  • If the repo or download has no buyer path at all, use the setup service instead of patching one small line at a time.
