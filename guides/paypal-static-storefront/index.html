The Minimum Static Storefront

A small static storefront does not need a cart or backend on day one. It does need enough trust signals that a buyer understands what they get and how delivery happens after payment.

Offer

One product, one promise

Name the deliverable, price, file format, update policy, and support boundary. A vague page loses buyers before the payment link matters.

Preview

A small public sample

Show a ZIP manifest, report excerpt, demo page, APKG sample, or checklist so the buyer can inspect quality without asking for private access.

Payment

A direct checkout path

Use the payment or store platform you already trust. Keep the button text price-specific and place support or delivery instructions nearby.

Delivery

A private follow-up channel

For manual delivery, send buyers to email after payment. Public issues should not ask for full transaction IDs, screenshots, credentials, or customer data.

Buyer-Readiness Checklist

  • Page URL returns HTTP 200 and loads on mobile.
  • Title, description, canonical, Open Graph, and Twitter metadata are present.
  • Payment button includes the exact price and goes to the expected PayPal or store URL.
  • Preview or sample link works without login.
  • Refund/support terms are visible before payment.
  • Delivery email or buyer-help form is close to the payment button.
  • Public forms avoid private payment details, credentials, and customer files.
  • Robots.txt and sitemap.xml expose the product page and guide pages.
